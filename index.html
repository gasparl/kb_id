Information and Consent

Study Goal:
The study aims to assess the validity of typing pattern based authentication. You will be asked to type in various texts, and your keypress details (the time of pressing down and letting up each key) will be recorded. Using the data obtained from many participants in this study, we will try to see whether or not we can identify the person who truly wrote a specific text section, based on the typing pattern of the same person in other text sections. In one part of the study, you will be asked to try to fake the way you type. In the rest of the study, you will be asked to type as you normally would.

Payment and Rules:
The test takes around 20-25 minutes. (Not counting any voluntary short breaks during testing, which you may take any time.) Completed and valid participation will be rewarded with $3.00 gross. You can stop participating in the study (by closing or refreshing the website) at any time, and without giving reasons. In that case, all your data will be erased, and you will not receive payment.

Instructions

Your task is as follows. You will see a short text in the middle of the screen. Memorize this text and press Enter. The text will then disappear and you have to type it into a text field from memory.

Example: (1) The text "the sky is blue" appears. (2) You press Enter. (3) The text "the sky is blue" disappears and a textbox shows up in which you have to type "the sky is blue".

Please try to be accurate. Minor typos, e.g. "bleu" or "blu" instead of "blue", are acceptable. However, if your typed text is very different from the original text, a warning screen will be displayed (and you may have to wait a few seconds before continuing).

Please use lowercase letters only. Copy-pasting is disabled, and you should not try using it.
