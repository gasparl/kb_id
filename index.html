Participation Information and Consent Form


Study Goal:
The study aims to assess the validity of typing pattern based authentication. You will be asked to type in various texts, and your keypress details (the time of pressing down and letting up each key) will be recorded. Using the data obtained from many participants in this study, we will try to see whether or not we can identify the person who truly wrote a specific text section, based on the typing pattern of the same person in other text sections. In one part of the study, you will be asked to try to fake the way you type. In the rest of the study, you will be asked to type as you normally would.


Payment and Rules:
The test takes around 20-25 minutes. Completed and valid participation will be rewarded with $3.00 gross. You can stop participating in the study (by closing or refreshing the website) at any time, and without giving reasons. In that case, all your data will be erased, and you will not receive payment.


Data:
The data from the following test will be used and published to be freely available for research purposes. The data will not reveal who you are, you will not be named and your identity will remain strictly confidential.

In the entire following test, on each trial you will see a word or a sentence. You will have to memorize this word or sentence and press Enter. The word or sentence will then disappear, and you have to type it from your memory.

Example: (1) The word "apple" appears. (2) You press enter. (3) The word "apple" disappears and you have a textbox where you have to type "apple".

Please try to be accurate. Occasional small mistakes (e.g. appel instead of apple) are acceptable, but if your typed text is very different from the original text, your will get a warning each time.

The following two trials will serve as practice, to get used to the task.



The remaining test will be the same, but it will consist of four sections. You will receive instructions before each section separately.

In this section, please type just as you normally would. This represents a scenario where you simply use the computer in a regular situation (e.g. drafting a document for your legal work or writing casual chat messages) and you have nothing important to hide.

In this section, please try to fake your way of typing. It is up to you how you do this. This represents a situation where you engage in a serious illegal activity while using your computer (e.g. drafting a plan for a terrorist attack or arranging a contract murder via chat messages) and it is important for you to hide your identity, which may be detected based on the way you type.
